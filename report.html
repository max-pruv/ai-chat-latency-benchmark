Key findings

Yuma has no chat widget of its own. On EvryJewels (and Glossier) the on-site chat is Gorgias Chat; the AI auto-reply rides the Gorgias chat WebSocket and arrives as one block (no token streaming). It is a back-end automation layer, not a standalone chat product — and the slowest to a full answer (avg ~14.3s, up to ~18s), with 1 of 7 questions getting no in-chat reply at all within 70s.
Ada was deployed but unavailable. Its bot returned "experiencing technical difficulties" on both EU and US Loop instances throughout the ~20-min window — an availability signal in itself. Sierra is fastest and the only one to stream tokens (first words in ~0.4s) and render interactive product cards with Add-to-Cart in chat. DigitalGenius (Bloom & Wild) gates its AI behind a name+email prechat form and escalated the product-recommendation question to a human — only Sierra, Siena and Yuma actually attempt in-chat product recommendations.

Latency by question type

The biggest driver of latency isn't the vendor — it's what you ask. Simple FAQs are fast; product-recommendation questions are consistently the slowest, because the agent has to retrieve over the catalog, reason about fit, and often render product cards.

What the per-conversation timings show:
  • Product recommendations cost the most. On the Gorgias demo a simple FAQ returns in ~6.4s, but "which product do you recommend?" took ~17s (≈2.7× slower) — it retrieves the catalog, picks SKUs, renders cards and applies a discount code.
  • Sierra is the outlier on recommendations (~8s) — it streams tokens (first words in ~0.4s) and uses a prebuilt product-card system, so a rec isn't much slower than an FAQ.
  • Siena and Yuma look "flat" by type only because they deliver the whole answer in one block — that masks the work, but keeps them in the ~10s (Siena) / ~14s (Yuma) range regardless.
  • DigitalGenius doesn't pay the rec cost — because it doesn't do it. It escalated the recommendation question to a human instead of answering.
  • Within a vendor, FAQ < policy < product-rec is the consistent ordering. Policy answers land in the middle (the Gorgias agent even appends a product upsell to its returns answer, pushing it to ~13.5s).

Sierra — richest commerce UI

Renders interactive product cards inline in chat — image, specs, a Select Size dropdown, Learn More and Add to Cart. On "which mattress for a hot sleeper?" it surfaced Snow & Snow Max cards. Closest to true in-chat shopping.

Methodology & caveats

Each widget was driven programmatically on the live customer site; the metric is wall-clock from message sent to the full response being available.

  • Siena — chat API: POST /v1/live_chat/messages then poll /v1/live_chat/message until the bot reply appears (delivered atomically).
  • Sierra — network layer: intercept the streaming endpoint sierra.chat/-/api/chat for first-byte and stream-close timestamps (throttle-immune).
  • Yuma — WebSocket layer: timestamp the first fromAgent message frame on the Gorgias chat socket after each send.
  • DigitalGenius — same-origin widget iframe: timestamp reply text growth + stability in the dg-chat-widget-iframe document. A throwaway test identity (reserved example.com email) was used to clear its prechat form.
  • Gorgias (us) — same-origin chat-window iframe on the NouriVida demo store: reply text growth + stability. Each question type (FAQ, policy, product-recommendation) was timed separately. Note this is Gorgias's own sales-tuned demo, so treat as best-case.

Caveats. Different customers / product domains (water bottles · mattresses · jewelry) → directional, not a controlled head-to-head. Sierra ran on a warm session (conservative). Latency varies by query type: simple FAQs (~4.6s Sierra) are far faster than retrieval-heavy product questions (~9.7s). Yuma collection was rate-limited to 3 turns. Ada could not be tested (backend down). Yuma vs Gorgias-AI attribution on EvryJewels is not verifiable client-side.
