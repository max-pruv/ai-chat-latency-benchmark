Key findings

Yuma has no chat widget of its own. On EvryJewels (and Glossier) the on-site chat is Gorgias Chat; the AI auto-reply rides the Gorgias chat WebSocket and arrives as one block (no token streaming). It is a back-end automation layer, not a standalone chat product — and the slowest to a full answer (avg ~14.3s, up to ~18s), with 1 of 7 questions getting no in-chat reply at all within 70s.
Ada was deployed but unavailable. Its bot returned "experiencing technical difficulties" on both EU and US Loop instances throughout the ~20-min window — an availability signal in itself. Sierra is fastest and the only one to stream tokens (first words in ~0.4s) and render interactive product cards with Add-to-Cart in chat.

Methodology & caveats

Each widget was driven programmatically on the live customer site; the metric is wall-clock from message sent to the full response being available.

  • Siena — chat API: POST /v1/live_chat/messages then poll /v1/live_chat/message until the bot reply appears (delivered atomically).
  • Sierra — network layer: intercept the streaming endpoint sierra.chat/-/api/chat for first-byte and stream-close timestamps (throttle-immune).
  • Yuma — WebSocket layer: timestamp the first fromAgent message frame on the Gorgias chat socket after each send.

Caveats. Different customers / product domains (water bottles · mattresses · jewelry) → directional, not a controlled head-to-head. Sierra ran on a warm session (conservative). Latency varies by query type: simple FAQs (~4.6s Sierra) are far faster than retrieval-heavy product questions (~9.7s). Yuma collection was rate-limited to 3 turns. Ada could not be tested (backend down). Yuma vs Gorgias-AI attribution on EvryJewels is not verifiable client-side.
